About Us

Built for Maurice.
Built for you.

C'est Bon Laundry began the way most meaningful things do, with one woman who saw a need and decided to do something about it. We are currently in our pre-launch phase, working diligently to bring a much needed laundry service to Vermilion Parish.


Families in Maurice and the surrounding community of Milton are leaving their community just to find a washateria. That's not a minor inconvenience for working families, that's lost time and money they can't afford. C'est Bon Laundry is here to change that.

"For me, community means showing up, filling gaps, and making sure people don't fall through the cracks."

— Founder, C'est Bon Laundry

The Real Cost

What home laundry actually costs.

Between the machines, the supplies, the energy, and your time, doing laundry at home carries a price most people never stop to add up.

⏱️
Your Time
1–8
hours per week
Single adults average 1–2 hours a week. Families with children spend 5–8 hours or more — a full workday, every week, just on laundry.
Source: BLS American Time Use Survey
⚡
Energy Costs
$120–$366
per year
Running a washer and dryer adds up fast. The dryer alone accounts for 82% of laundry electricity use — and older machines cost even more to run.
Source: ENERGY STAR / U.S. Dept. of Energy
🧴
Supplies
$85–$300
per year
Detergent, fabric softener, dryer sheets, stain removers — small purchases that quietly add up to hundreds of dollars every single year.
Source: Statista / BLS Consumer Expenditure Survey

Machine Wear
$150–$290
per year
Your washer and dryer depreciate with every cycle. Add routine repairs and eventual replacement and you're spending $150–$290 a year just to keep the machines running.
Source: Consumer Reports / Fixr / Angi

Our Story

Rooted in community.

C'est Bon Laundry was founded by a woman deeply rooted in community service. As a CASA volunteer — a Court Appointed Special Advocate for children in the welfare system — and someone who has spent years as an autism advocate, this business is a natural extension of that commitment.


For me, community means showing up, filling gaps, and making sure people don't fall through the cracks. C'est Bon Laundry is built on that same belief.
