# Resume anti-patterns

What gets resumes silently rejected. Run this checklist against every draft before delivery.

## Formatting that breaks ATS parsing

- Tables, text boxes, multi-column layouts
- Images, photos, logos, headshots, icons
- Skill bars, dot-rating graphics, progress charts
- Header/footer for contact info (parsers often skip these)
- Custom or decorative fonts (Comic Sans, Papyrus, anything script)
- Colored backgrounds, shading, watermarks
- Text inside shapes or SmartArt
- Embedded hyperlinks without the readable URL also in text
- Non-standard bullet characters (emoji, Wingdings, ★, ►)
- Date formats other than `Month YYYY – Month YYYY`

## Banned phrases (clichés and filler)

- "Results-driven", "results-oriented"
- "Hardworking", "hard worker"
- "Team player"
- "Detail-oriented"
- "Go-getter"
- "Dynamic"
- "Synergy", "synergistic"
- "Self-starter"
- "Passionate about"
- "Proven track record"
- "Thinks outside the box"
- "Out-of-the-box thinker"
- "Strategic thinker"
- "Responsible for"
- "Duties included"
- "Tasked with"
- "Helped to" / "Assisted with" / "Worked on"
- "Various" (as in "various projects")
- "Etc." anywhere

If any of these appear, rewrite the bullet to show the behavior with a specific quantified action instead.

## Content red flags recruiters reject on

- **No metrics.** If <50% of bullets have a number, the resume reads as task-list, not impact-list.
- **Generic summary.** A summary that could appear on 1,000 other resumes signals mass-application behavior.
- **Listing responsibilities, not achievements.** "Managed a team of 5" is a job duty. "Led 5-person team that cut cycle time 30%" is an achievement.
- **Unexplained employment gaps.** A 2+ month gap with no context invites suspicion. Add a one-liner: "Career break — caregiving / sabbatical / upskilling (Coursera ML cert)".
- **Functional / skills-first format.** Reads as "hiding something". Use reverse-chronological.
- **Inconsistency with LinkedIn.** Recruiters cross-check. Titles, dates, and companies should match exactly.
- **Typos and grammar errors.** ~59% of recruiters reject on a single typo. Spell-check is non-negotiable.
- **Too long.** >1 page for <10 years experience or >2 pages ever (excluding academic CVs).
- **Too short.** A half-page resume signals lack of experience even when the candidate has it; expand bullets or add Projects.
- **One-size-fits-all.** A resume not tailored to the target role is rejected by both ATS and humans.
- **Photo / DOB / marital status / nationality** in US/UK/CA markets. Not standard, can trigger anti-bias screening.
- **References available upon request.** Wastes a line; assumed.
- **Personal pronouns.** "I led a team" → "Led 6-person team".
- **Articles where optional.** "Led a team of 6" → "Led 6-person team".

## Structural red flags

- Reverse-chronological order broken (a 2018 role above a 2022 role)
- Date overlaps unexplained
- Job titles that don't match the company's actual title for the role
- Education before Experience for candidates with >2 years of work experience
- Certifications dumped into the summary instead of their own section
- Skills section that's just a wall of 60+ comma-separated items
- "Hobbies" section longer than 2 lines (or present at all unless directly relevant to the role)
