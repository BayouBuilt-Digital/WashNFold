The Hidden Cost

Hours your team shouldn't be spending on laundry.

In-house laundry looks like a small task until you add it up. Here's what businesses like yours are actually spending — and what they get back when they hand it off.

🏫
Daycare
3–7
staff hours / week

A 30-child center runs 8–12 loads a week just to meet state hygiene requirements — bibs after every feeding, bedding weekly, washcloths daily. That's 3–7 hours of staff time that belongs in the classroom.

🍽️
Restaurant
5–10
staff hours / week

A single kitchen shift generates aprons, chef coats, and up to 24 rags. Industry estimates put in-house laundry at 8–10 hours per week for a small restaurant — essentially a part-time position consumed by the wash cycle.

🏋️
Gym
5–15
staff hours / week

A gym with 100 daily members goes through roughly 700 towels a week — 9 to 10 machine loads. One fitness center reported front desk staff spending 3 hours a day on towels alone, pulling directly from member-facing coverage.

💇
Salon
5–9
staff hours / week

A busy 4-chair salon washes 450–540 towels a week. When a stylist handles laundry, they're not just spending time — they're spending revenue. At $75 per service, two hours a week in the laundry room costs a salon $300+ in lost earnings.

20–40
staff hours reclaimed every month

That's time back for training, client care, operations, and everything else that actually moves your business forward. Let us handle the laundry.
